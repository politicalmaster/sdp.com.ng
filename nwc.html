Hajia Maggie Batubo

National Women Leader, SDP

Maggie Mariam Batubo, the National Women Leader of the Social Democratic Party of Nigeria, was born in Buguma in 1962 in Asari Toru Local Government Area of Rivers State.

She attended the Township School, Works (now Moscow) Road, Port Harcourt, obtaining her First School Leaving Certificate in 1976.

She obtained a Bachelor of Science degree (B.Sc. Hon) in Sociology from the University of Port Harcourt in 2007.

She has been engaged as a professional caterer, running the “3 Meals Catering Company,” a vibrant catering service business for about three decades now, having honed her skills at the Delta Hotels Catering School in Port Harcourt.

In 2003, she ventured into politics, joining the Movement for the Restoration and Defence of Democracy (MRDD). Her contributions in this movement propelled her to the position of the Rivers State Women Leader. Her skills at social mobilization have stood her in good stead in her political activities.

Maggie Batubo was a founding member of the Niger Delta People’s Salvation Front (NDPSF), leading the Rivers State front until 2006, when she went on to join the Democratic People’s Party (DPP) of Nigeria, again rising to the position of Rivers State Women Leader.

Contributing to the efforts to forge the Social Democratic Mega Party in 2011, she emerged as the National Women Leader. She was elected the National Women Leader of the Social Democratic Party (SDP) in 2013, a position which she has held till date, and in which she is exhibiting a remarkable performance.
